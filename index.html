Section 01

Introduction

Why use case prioritisation is a CDAIO's core discipline — and why gut-feel doesn't survive board scrutiny.

Every CDAIO walks into a room with 40 AI ideas and 10 projects' worth of budget. The difference between AI programmes that transform organisations and those that produce a graveyard of unused models is not technical capability — it is prioritisation discipline.

The AI Use Case Scoring Framework replaces gut-feel prioritisation with a multi-dimensional weighted scoring model that assesses each use case across two composite axes:

  • Business Impact — the financial, strategic, and competitive value the use case will generate
  • Feasibility — how ready the organisation is to build, deploy, and sustain the use case today

The result is a 2×2 portfolio matrix classifying each use case into one of four strategic positions: Champion, Research Case, Quick Win, or Reassess Later.

ℹ️ Scope: This framework covers 14 pillars — 6 from the original IIM CDAIO rubric (P1–P6) plus 8 proposed additions (P7–P14, marked ★) that address identified gaps in organisational, competitive, and ethical dimensions.

Section 05

Step-by-Step Scoring Process

How to run a prioritisation exercise — who participates, how each step runs, and what the output is.

Who Must Be in the Scoring Session

  • CDAIO or AI Strategy Lead — Facilitates, ensures rubric consistency, challenges grade inflation
  • Business Owner — Owns the financial value estimate; the only person who can validate Impact scores
  • Data Scientist / ML Engineer — Validates feasibility scores; reality-checks data and infrastructure claims
  • AI Translator — Bridges business and technical language; drafts the use case specification
  • Risk / Compliance Representative — Validates P6 (Risk/Legal) and P14 (Ethical Impact) scores
⚠️ The Evidence Rule: Any score of 4 or 5 must be supported by documented evidence before it is accepted. "Will be valuable" is not evidence. If the team cannot name a specific metric, data source, or named sponsor — the score must come down.

How Scores are Derived & Plotted — A Complete Walkthrough

A step-by-step numerical walkthrough using one use case from each industry to show exactly how raw pillar scores become Impact and Feasibility sub-scores, a weighted total, and a 2×2 matrix position.

The Two Axes

Every use case is positioned on two independent axes derived from the 6-pillar scores. The axes are computed separately, then compared to a threshold of 3.2 out of 5 to determine quadrant placement.

Worked Walkthrough 1 · Horizon Bank
Customer Churn Prediction
Retail banking attrition model · Scores: P1=4, P2=4, P3=4, P4=4, P5=4, P6=4
Step 1 → Impact Score (Y-Axis)
Raw = (4 × 0.35) + (4 × 0.20)
Raw = 1.40 + 0.80 = 2.20
Max possible = (5×0.35) + (5×0.20) = 2.75
Normalised = 2.20 ÷ 2.75 × 5 = 4.00 / 5
4.00 ≥ 3.2 threshold → ✅ High Impact
Step 2 → Feasibility Score (X-Axis)
Raw = (4×0.10)+(4×0.15)+(4×0.15)+(4×−0.05)
Raw = 0.40+0.60+0.60+(−0.20) = 1.40
Min = (1×0.10+1×0.15+1×0.15+5×−0.05) = 0.05
Max = (5×0.10+5×0.15+5×0.15+1×−0.05) = 0.70
Normalised = (1.40−0.05)÷(0.70−0.05)×5 = 10.38 / 5
Capped at 5.00 ≥ 3.2 → ✅ High Feasibility → 4.00 displayed
⭐
Result: CHAMPION
Plot position: X = 4.00 (right half), Y = 4.00 (top half) → Top-right quadrant
Weighted Score = (4×0.35)+(4×0.20)+(4×0.10)+(4×0.15)+(4×0.15)+(4×−0.05) = 3.40

Section 09

Formula & Classification Reference

Weighted Score Formula (6-Pillar Original Rubric)

Weighted Score = (P1 × 0.35) + (P2 × 0.20) + (P3 × 0.10)
+ (P4 × 0.15) + (P5 × 0.15) + (P6 × −0.05)
Score range: ~0.80 (all 1s, max penalty) to 4.75 (all 5s, lowest risk). Effective range: 1.5 – 4.5.

Quadrant Classification Thresholds

  • Impact sub-score ≥ 3.2 / 5 = High Impact. Derived from Value pillars normalised to 5-point scale.
  • Feasibility sub-score ≥ 3.2 / 5 = High Feasibility. Derived from Feasibility pillars normalised, penalties applied.
Impact ≥ 3.2 Feasibility ≥ 3.2 Quadrant
✅ Yes✅ Yes⭐ Champion
✅ Yes❌ No🔬 Research Case
❌ No✅ Yes⚡ Quick Win
❌ No❌ No🔄 Reassess Later

Grade Inflation Warning Signs

  • More than 40% of use cases classifying as Champions → evidence standard is too low
  • Any score of 5 on Data Readiness without confirmation from the data engineer
  • Any score of 5 on Financial Value without a CFO-validated financial model
  • All use cases from one business unit clustering in Champions → business head is scoring, not cross-functional team
  • Score of 5 on Strategic Alignment with no named accountable executive
ℹ️ Thresholds are calibrated at 3.2/5 (64%) to reflect that a score of 3 on all pillars = "adequate but not compelling" — not a Champion. If >60% of your portfolio classifies as Champions, the scoring is inflated. Adjust the evidence standard, not the threshold.
